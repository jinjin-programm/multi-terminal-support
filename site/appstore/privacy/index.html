App Store · Effective July 16, 2026

Privacy

The App Store product is local-first. Workspace, terminal history, and settings are kept on the Mac rather than in a Multi-Terminal cloud account.

Folders and security-scoped bookmarks

You choose which folders the app may access. macOS security-scoped bookmarks let the sandboxed app remember permission to reopen a selected folder. Those permission references are stored locally with the app's workspace data.

StoreKit subscriptions

Apple processes purchases and payment details through StoreKit. The app reads StoreKit transaction and entitlement status and keeps a local offline entitlement cache so it can determine access when appropriate. Multi-Terminal does not collect your payment card details.

Accounts and backend

The App Store product does not require a Multi-Terminal, Supabase, GitHub, or Google login and does not use a project-operated account backend for subscription access.

Terminal activity

Commands you run can access files and the network with the permissions available to the sandboxed app. Any external command or service you choose to use has its own data practices.

Support pages and issues

No analytics script or support form has been added to these static pages. GitHub hosts the pages and public issue tracker under its own terms and privacy practices. Public issue content is public; never post receipts, credentials, private paths, or sensitive terminal output.
